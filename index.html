PROSODY PRE-TRAINING FOR IMPROVING EXPRESSIVENESS IN NEURAL TTS

Authors: Liping Chen, Yan Deng, Shifeng Pan, Xi Wang, Frank K. Soong, Lei He (Submitted to ICASSP 2020)
Abstract: Based upon the existing TTS speech BERT model, the expressiveness of the corresponding neural text-to-speech (TTS) is further improved in this study. A prosody prediction module is proposed to be trained in a multi-task training set-up, along with other acoustic information prediction in the original speech BERT, to enhance the TTS prosody modeling. The speech encoder extracts prosody embedding which carries the prosody related information like pitch, voicing and pause in the speech segments. Experimental results show that, by incorporating the prosody embedding in Transformer-based TTS, the expressiveness of TTS speech is improved. Subjective MOS listening tests conducted on LJSpeech database also show that the synthesized speech sounds more natural than the original speech BERT baseline. Preference listening tests confirm that the model trained with the prosody embedding is preferred over the baseline. The results are valid for both in- and out-of-domain scenarios.
